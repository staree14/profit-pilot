# Government of India MSME Schemes & Financing Guidelines (2025-26)

This document provides a structured summary of active government funding, credit assistance, and operational development schemes managed by the Ministry of Micro, Small, and Medium Enterprises, Government of India.

---

## 1. Credit & Financing Schemes

### A. Prime Minister’s Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP)
* **Objective**: Credit-linked subsidy for setting up new micro-enterprises in non-farm sectors.
* **Funding Cap**: 
  * Up to **₹50 Lakh** for manufacturing projects.
  * Up to **₹20 Lakh** for service sector projects.
* **Subsidy Tiers**:
  * *General Category*: 15% (urban), 25% (rural). Beneficiary contribution: 10%.
  * *Special Categories* (SC/ST/OBC/Women/Minorities/NER/PwD): 25% (urban), 35% (rural). Beneficiary contribution: 5%.
* **Eligibility**: Any individual above 18 years of age.
* **Second Upgradation Loan**: Existing well-performing PMEGP/MUDRA units can apply for a second loan for technological expansion/upgradation:
  * Maximum cost: **₹1.00 Crore** (manufacturing), **₹25.00 Lakh** (service/trading).
  * Maximum subsidy: 15% (20% for NER/Hill States) i.e. up to ₹15 Lakh (Non-NER) or ₹20 Lakh (NER).

### B. Credit Guarantee Scheme for Micro & Small Enterprises (CGTMSE)
* **Objective**: Provision of collateral-free and third-party guarantee-free credit facilities to micro and small enterprises (MSEs).
* **Ceiling Limit**: Guarantee cover for loans up to **₹5.00 Crore**.
* **Guarantee Coverage**: Ranges from **75% to 90%** of the loan value:
  * *Micro Enterprises*: 85% for loans up to ₹5 Lakh, 75% for loans above ₹5 Lakh.
  * *Women-Owned Enterprises*: 90% coverage.
  * *SC/ST Entrepreneurs / Aspirational Districts / ZED Certified MSEs*: 85% coverage.
  * *NER / Ladakh / J&K*: 80% coverage.

### C. Self Reliant India (SRI) Fund
* **Objective**: Multi-tiered growth capital support through a Mother-Daughter fund structure.
* **Corpus**: **₹10,000 Crore** contributed by the Government of India.
* **Instruments**: Equity, quasi-equity, and debt-equity hybrid structures.
* **Target Group**: Viable MSMEs with positive growth trajectories preparing for listing on stock exchanges.

## 2. Technology, Intellectual Property & Quality Schemes

### A. MSME Champions Scheme: Sustainable (ZED) Certification
* **Objective**: Encourage Zero Defect Zero Effect (ZED) manufacturing practices.
* **Certification Subsidies**:
  * *Micro*: 80% subsidy.
  * *Small*: 60% subsidy.
  * *Medium*: 50% subsidy.
  * *Women-Owned / NER*: **100% subsidy**.
* **Financial Assistance**:
  * Up to **₹50,000** (75% support) for testing/quality/product certification costs.
  * Up to **₹2 Lakh** handholding support for ZED consultants.
  * Up to **₹3 Lakh** for technology upgradation to achieve zero environmental effect.

### B. MSME Champions Scheme: Innovative (Incubation, IPR & Design)
* **Incubation Support**: Up to **₹15 Lakh** per innovative idea to Host Institutions (HI), and up to **₹1.00 Crore** for machinery installation.
* **Design Support**: Contribution of 75% (Micro) or 60% (Small & Medium) of project cost up to a maximum of **₹40 Lakh** for design projects.
* **Intellectual Property Rights (IPR) Assistance**:
  * Up to **₹1 Crore** to establish IP Facilitation Centers (IPFC).
  * *Reimbursements for Registration*:
    * Foreign Patent: Up to **₹5.00 Lakh**.
    * Domestic Patent: Up to **₹1.00 Lakh**.
    * GI Registration: Up to **₹2.00 Lakh**.
    * Design Registration: Up to **₹15,000**.
    * Trademark: Up to **₹10,000**.

### C. MSME Champions Scheme: Competitive (LEAN) Scheme
* **Objective**: Reduction of manufacturing waste, optimization of space, water, energy, and enhancement of workplace safety.
* **Subsidy**: **90% GoI contribution** on implementation costs.
  * *Intermediate Level*: Maximum subsidy of ₹1.08 Lakh (Beneficiary pays ₹12,000).
  * *Advanced Level*: Maximum subsidy of ₹2.16 Lakh (Beneficiary pays ₹24,000).

## 3. Marketing, Infrastructure & Late Payment Mitigation

### A. Procurement and Marketing Support (PMS) Scheme
* **Objective**: Assist MSEs in accessing new domestic and international markets.
* **Financial Limits (Sub-Component I)**:
  * Space/Stall Rent: Up to **₹3.00 Lakh** per MSME.
  * Air Travel: Up to **₹1.50 Lakh** per MSME.
  * Capacity Building: Subsidizes adoption of modern packaging, bar coding, and onboarding on e-commerce platforms.

### B. Raising and Accelerating MSME Performance (RAMP)
* **Objective**: World Bank-supported initiative to improve credit access, accelerate technology integration, and strengthen receivable financing markets.
* **Late Payment Reductions**: Provides structural and institutional assistance to reduce the incidence of delayed payments and dispute settlement periods for MSMEs.

### C. Scheme for Promotion of Innovation, Rural Industries & Entrepreneurship (ASPIRE)
* **Objective**: Create a network of Livelihood Business Incubators (LBI) for employment generation in agro-rural sectors.
* **Financial Assistance**:
  * Up to **₹1.00 Crore** for procuring plant and machinery for government-run LBIs (₹75 Lakh for private LBIs).
  * Up to **₹1.00 Crore** for operational expenditure (manpower, incubation running).
