# Customer Concentration and Retention Strategy Guide

## 1. What is Customer Concentration Risk?
Customer Concentration Risk occurs when a significant portion of an SME's revenue is generated by a single customer or a small group of customers.
- **The 20% Threshold**: Industry standard defines concentration risk when a single client represents **more than 20%** of total revenue, or when the top 3 clients represent more than 50% of revenue.
- **Vulnerability**: If a major client goes bankrupt, shifts to a competitor, or renegotiates pricing downwards, the SME's profitability and solvency are immediately threatened.
- **Squeezed Margins**: Concentrated customers have extreme bargaining power and will often demand deep discounts, late payment terms, or custom work that drives up delivery costs.

## 2. Strategies to Mitigate Concentration Risk
- **Diversification Program**: Set a strategic goal to acquire smaller accounts in different verticals or geographies to spread risk.
- **Referral and Incentive Programs**: Encourage existing medium-sized accounts to refer others, offering discounts on future services rather than current invoices.
- **Productization**: Turn custom services into standard packages. This allows self-service purchases and reduces dependance on bespoke sales to giant accounts.
- **Account Expansion**: Lock key customers into long-term retainer agreements or multi-year contracts rather than relying on purchase orders (POs) which can be cancelled at any time.
- **Cost-to-Serve Auditing**: Calculate the exact profit margin of your top customers. Often, when custom delivery costs, support hours, and discount rates are counted, the top customer is actually a low-margin account.
