# SME Inventory and Cost Control Guide

## 1. Inventory Holding Costs
Holding inventory incurs silent costs that erode an SME's net profit margin:
- **Capital Lock-up**: Money tied up in unsold goods cannot be used to pay off short-term debts or invest in marketing.
- **Storage and Warehousing**: Fees for space, security, utilities, and insurance.
- **Shrinkage and Obsolescence**: Risk of products expiring, going out of style, or getting damaged.
- **Rule of Thumb**: Annual inventory holding costs typically run between **20% and 30%** of the total inventory value.

## 2. Optimizing Stock Levels and Cost Controls
- **ABC Analysis**: Classify inventory into three categories:
  - *Class A*: High-value products with low sales frequency (require strict control and low safety stock).
  - *Class B*: Medium-value products with moderate sales frequency.
  - *Class C*: Low-value products with high sales frequency (require less control, higher safety stock to avoid stockouts).
- **Just-In-Time (JIT) Stocking**: Work with local suppliers to coordinate frequent, smaller shipments to reduce warehouse footprints, provided supplier lead times are reliable.
- **Min-Max Stock Levels**: Set automated threshold alerts in inventory software. When stock falls below the minimum level, a purchase order is triggered up to the maximum level.
- **Supplier Negotiations**: Renegotiate minimum order quantities (MOQs) with suppliers. High MOQs force SMEs to hold excess inventory, causing cash flow problems.
- **Margin Monitoring**: Conduct regular checks on unit costs vs selling prices. If raw material prices spike, selling prices must be adjusted to maintain target margins.
