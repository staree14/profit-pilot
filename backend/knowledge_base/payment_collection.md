# SME Credit Control and Cash Flow Management Guide

## 1. The Impact of Late Payments on Profitability
Late payments increase the Accounts Receivable (AR) collection period, locking up cash that could be used for operating capital. For SMEs, high Days Sales Outstanding (DSO) leads to:
- **Cash Flow Crises**: Difficulty paying suppliers or staff on time, requiring costly short-term loans or lines of credit.
- **Administrative Costs**: The time and resources spent chasing late invoices represent a direct drain on profitability.
- **Bad Debt Write-offs**: The longer an invoice remains unpaid, the higher the probability it becomes uncollectible, leading to direct write-offs.

## 2. Best Practices for Timely Collection
- **Clear Credit Terms**: Establish and write credit terms clearly on every invoice and contract (e.g., Net 30, Net 15). Never leave terms ambiguous.
- **Early Payment Discounts**: Offer small incentives for early settlement, such as a "2/10 Net 30" term, where the customer receives a 2% discount if they pay within 10 days, otherwise full payment is due in 30 days.
- **Automated Reminders**: Use accounting software to send automated polite email reminders 7 days before due date, on the due date, and 7, 15, and 30 days post-due date.
- **Interest and Late Fees**: Include a clause in contracts reserving the right to charge interest on late invoices (e.g., 1.5% per month or statutory interest). While rarely enforced, this acts as a strong deterrent.
- **Upfront Deposits / Milestone Payments**: For large orders or services, require a 30-50% upfront deposit or establish milestone-based billing to reduce credit exposure.
- **Credit Checks**: Vet new commercial accounts before offering net payment terms to ensure they have a history of creditworthiness.
